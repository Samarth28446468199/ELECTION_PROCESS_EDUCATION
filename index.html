Election Commission of India

The Guardian of Indian Democracy

The ECI is an autonomous constitutional authority responsible for administering Union and State election processes in India.

⚖️

Constitutional Body

Established under Article 324 of the Indian Constitution. It is independent of the government to ensure free and fair elections.

👨‍⚖️

Chief Election Commissioner

Led by the CEC, appointed by the President of India. Can only be removed via a process similar to a Supreme Court judge.

📋

Model Code of Conduct

The MCC kicks in from election announcement. Restricts the ruling government from making policy announcements that could influence voters.

🗓️

Election Schedule

ECI announces election dates, polling phases, candidate filing deadlines, and result dates. Lok Sabha elections occur in multiple phases.

Step by Step

How Indian Elections Work

From announcement to oath-taking — the complete Indian election journey.

1
📢

Election Announcement

ECI announces the election schedule. The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) comes into effect immediately. Political parties cannot announce new schemes or make promises that may influence voters.

MCC BeginsECI Notifies
2
📋

Voter Registration

Citizens aged 18+ register via Form 6 on the National Voters' Service Portal (NVSP) or at local ERO offices. The Electoral Photo Identity Card (EPIC) / Voter ID is issued.

Form 6NVSP PortalEPIC Card
3
📝

Nomination Filing

Candidates file nomination papers with the Returning Officer. They must submit an affidavit declaring criminal background, assets, and educational qualifications. A security deposit is mandatory.

Affidavit RequiredSecurity DepositParty Symbol
4
📣

Campaign Period

Parties campaign through rallies, social media, and door-to-door outreach. Campaign spending is capped (₹95 lakhs per candidate for Lok Sabha). Campaigning stops 48 hours before polling — the Silent Period.

Spending Cap48hr Silent Period
5
✅

Polling Day

Voters visit their assigned booth and vote using the Electronic Voting Machine (EVM). They press the button next to their chosen candidate. The Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail (VVPAT) shows a paper slip for 7 seconds.

EVMVVPATInk MarkSecret
6
🔢

Vote Counting

EVM results are counted in the presence of candidates and observers. Results declared constituency-by-constituency. NOTA (None of the Above) votes are also counted separately.

NOTAObservedCertified
7
👑

Government Formation

The party/coalition with majority (272+ seats) forms the government. The President invites the leader to form the government. The Prime Minister and Cabinet are sworn in.

India's Electoral System

Types of Elections in India

🏛️

Lok Sabha (General) Election

Election for the lower house of Parliament. 543 constituencies across India. Held every 5 years. Winner forms central government.

Every 5 Years · 543 Seats
🏢

Rajya Sabha Election

Upper house election. Members elected by State Legislative Assembly members (MLAs), NOT directly by voters. 238 elected + 12 nominated.

Indirect Election · 250 Seats
🏙️

State Legislative Assembly (Vidhan Sabha)

State-level elections for MLAs. Each state has its own assembly. Parties need majority to form state government.

Every 5 Years · State-level
🏘️

Panchayat / Municipal Elections

Local body elections — Gram Panchayat, Nagar Palika, Municipal Corporation. Most direct impact on daily life. Conducted by State Election Commissions.

Every 5 Years · Local Level
🔄

By-Elections (Upchunav)

Held when a seat falls vacant mid-term. Separate election only for that constituency. Uses same EVM process.

As Needed
🎯

Presidential Election

President elected by Electoral College — elected MPs and MLAs. Votes are weighted. President is constitutional head of state.

Every 5 Years · Indirect

🔒

Tamper-Proof

EVMs are standalone machines, not connected to internet or any network. They cannot be hacked remotely.

📄

VVPAT Paper Trail

After pressing EVM, a paper slip appears in VVPAT window for 7 seconds, confirming your vote before it's sealed.

🔋

Battery Powered

Runs on 6V alkaline battery — works even in areas with no electricity. Can record up to 2,000 votes.

⚪

NOTA Option

None of the Above — added in 2013 by Supreme Court order. Allows voters to reject all candidates.

Myth vs Fact

Indian Election Myths Busted

❌ MYTH

"EVMs can be hacked."

✅ FACT

EVMs are not connected to any network. They're standalone machines. The Supreme Court and ECI have repeatedly upheld their integrity. VVPAT provides a paper audit trail.

❌ MYTH

"You need to be educated to vote."

✅ FACT

There is NO educational qualification to vote in India. Any Indian citizen 18+ can vote regardless of literacy. EVM has candidate photos and party symbols for easy identification.

❌ MYTH

"I can vote from anywhere in India."

✅ FACT

You must vote in the constituency where you're registered. However, you can transfer your registration if you've moved. Postal ballot is available for specific categories.

❌ MYTH

"Only Voter ID card is accepted at booth."

✅ FACT

12 alternate documents are accepted including Aadhaar, Passport, PAN Card, MNREGA card, and Bank/Post Office passbook with photo.

❌ MYTH

"NOTA means the seat stays empty."

✅ FACT

NOTA has no electoral value — the candidate with the highest votes still wins even if NOTA gets more votes. But it's a powerful symbolic protest vote.

❌ MYTH

"Voting takes too long, not worth it."

✅ FACT

Modern polling booths in India are highly organized. Average voting time is under 15 minutes. Employers must give paid leave on election day by law.
